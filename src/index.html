About Us

Seed (noun - /siːd/) - a small, [round or oval] object produced by a plant and from which, when it is planted, a new plant can grow.

What's the purpose of a seed? Logically and simply it's to birth a tree. But if you think of the value of a tree, you may find different and several meanings. It makes shadow so one can rest in a warm day. It gives shelter for birds on a stormy day. It may produce fruits. It protects rivers and lakes for natural development.

For our Seed Challenge we decided to look on how we could generate value from a small, symbolic contribution. We definitely want to make good, and help others. But how can we maximize the value of our actions?

Should we help those in need? Should we protect our environment? Should we invest on those creating a better future?

Instead of "just doing what we can", we have decided to invest the seed money offered to our team, and ask the support of our community, to create value to all parts involved. With this respect, we introduce Tomorrow's Wave, a ripple effect to fortify the "butterfly effect" of Tomorrow University.

Tomorrow's Wave aims to maximize the power of the community, by asking our fellowship for contributions and donations to sustainable and environmentally-friend NGOs, harnessing the power of the helping hands.

Ideally, the single fact we are supporting NGOs involved and actively protecting our environment, and developing sustainable actions to scale, should be good enough. But we aim for more: we want to, through this channel and the batch execution, to bring inventors, innovators and founders to mentor and lecture in our cohort.

We will collect donations monthly, and as we achieve our deadline, forward the accumulated to an NGO, fostering our involvement in their community, introducing ourselves, and possibly requesting for partnership.

The NGO wins, they will get a new, consistent donation. Tomorrow University wins, as the Purple Butterfly will be exposed and involved. Our Cohort i8 wins, as we might have the chance of founders, inventors, innovators and experts participating as mentors or lecturers in our program.

Tomorrow's Wave by Alina Braun, Benjamin Richards, Henrique Mattos and Minju Lee.
